A lone figure trudges through the neon-lit alleyways of a sprawling future city, the rain cascading in sheets over glistening concrete and rusted metal. In this movie, which doesn’t quite know if it wants to be a mystery, a romance, or a quiet meditation on memory, we follow a protagonist whose face we rarely see clearly—half-lit, smoke-wreathed, always looking for something just out of reach. The soundtrack hums with synths and echoes, giving weight to conversations that barely say anything yet feel important. There’s a subplot involving a forgotten photograph, a strange bird in a cage, and a recurring dream about the ocean. Everyone seems to speak in cryptic metaphors, and no one answers questions directly. The camera lingers a little too long, and time slows down as you wonder if something is about to happen—or if it already did and you missed it. There are long silences that say more than the dialogue, and when the credits roll, you’re not sure what the movie was about, but you kind of want to watch it again..
